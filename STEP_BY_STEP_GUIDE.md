2. Inside it, create three subfolders: `TO_FILE`, `FILED_CONFIRMATIONS`, `SENT_EMAILS`.

### 0.2 Turn a draft into a filable PDF (repeat for each document)
The drafts are text files on GitHub. To file one:
1. Open the draft (e.g., `filings/15_Designation_to_Court_Reporter.md`) on GitHub →
   click **Raw** → select all → copy.
2. Paste into **Google Docs** (free) or Word.
3. **DELETE the entire instructions block** (everything between the `<!-- -->` marks
   at top and bottom).
4. Fill every `[BRACKET]`. If a bracket can't be filled yet (like the
   transcriptionist name), the doc's instructions say what's allowed.
5. Clean up: the text should look like a court paper — centered caption is nice but
   not required; 12-pt or larger font; no weird symbols left over.
6. **Sign it:** print → sign in ink → scan or photograph clearly → save as PDF.
   (Phone scan is fine: Google Drive app → ➕ → Scan.) If you cannot print, typing
   `/s/ Brandi Rodgers` on the signature line is accepted for e-filed documents —
   but ink-sign the *verified* motions (01) if at all possible.
7. Name the PDF clearly: `15_Designation_Rodgers_2026CC001256.pdf` → save in `TO_FILE`.

### 0.3 Register on the e-filing portal (10 min, once)
1. Go to **myflcourtaccess.com** → click **Register** → choose **Self-Represented
   Litigant** account type.
2. Enter your mom's name exactly as it appears in the case (**Brandi Rodgers**), her
   address (13721 94th Ave., Seminole, FL 33776), and email
   (brandisreallife@gmail.com — filings must come from HER identity).
3. Confirm the account from the email link. Log in. You're set — the portal files to
   **both** the Pasco County Court **and** the Second DCA.

### 0.4 Pull the two appendix documents you'll need (5 min)
Both are already in this repo under `court_record/rodgers_filings/`:
