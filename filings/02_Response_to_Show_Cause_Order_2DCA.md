## APPELLANT'S RESPONSE TO ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE
### (Response to this Court's Order of June 26, 2026)

Appellant, BRANDI RODGERS, appearing pro se, responds to this Court's June 26, 2026
order directing her to show cause why this appeal should not be dismissed for lack of
jurisdiction, and states:

1. **The order appealed.** This appeal seeks review of the **Final Judgment for
Possession** entered by the County Court of the Sixth Judicial Circuit in and for Pasco
County, Florida (Hon. Kent Compton, County Court Judge) on **June 18, 2026**, in Case
No. 2026-CC-001256 (Division 6-B). **A copy of the Final Judgment is attached as
Appendix A**, curing the omission identified in the Court's order.

2. **The notice of appeal was timely.** Appellant filed her Notice of Appeal with the
clerk of the lower tribunal on **June 24, 2026** — six days after rendition of the Final
Judgment — within the 30 days required by Florida Rule of Appellate Procedure 9.110(b).
The lower tribunal clerk transmitted the notice to this Court, which acknowledged the
new case on June 26, 2026 as "NOA Final – County Civil – Landlord/Tenant/Eviction
(Residential)."

2a. **Any classification confusion originated in the transmittal, not the appeal.**
The lower tribunal clerk's Notice of Appeal Transmittal Form (DIN 50) appears to have
categorized this civil appeal under a criminal appeal classification and noted that a
copy of the order appealed did not accompany the notice. This Court's own
Acknowledgment of New Case (DIN 51), however, correctly identified the matter as "NOA
Final – County Civil – Landlord/Tenant/Eviction (Residential)." A copy of the
Acknowledgment is attached as **Appendix B**, and the order appealed is supplied as
Appendix A, curing the transmittal omission.

3. **This Court has jurisdiction.** This is an appeal from a final judgment of a county
court. Effective January 1, 2021, chapter 2020-61, Laws of Florida, amended section
26.012, Florida Statutes, to remove the circuit courts' general appellate jurisdiction
over county court judgments; appeals from county court final orders now lie in the
district courts of appeal. See § 26.012(1), Fla. Stat.; Fla. R. App. P. 9.030(b)(1)(A);
Fla. R. App. P. 9.110(a)(1). Jurisdiction of this appeal is therefore properly in this
Court.

4. **The reference to the circuit court in the notice was a pro se drafting error.**
Appellant's Notice of Appeal stated that she "appeals to the Circuit Court of the Sixth
Judicial Circuit." That designation reflected Appellant's self-represented
misunderstanding of the pre-2021 appellate routing, not an intent to invoke a different
remedy. The notice in all other respects correctly identified the order appealed (the
June 18, 2026 Final Judgment for Possession), the date of rendition, the lower tribunal
and case number, and the parties, and it invoked Florida Rule of Appellate Procedure
9.110.

5. **A misdesignation in the notice is not a jurisdictional defect requiring
dismissal.** Under Rule 9.040(d), "[a]t any time in the interest of justice, the court
may permit any part of the proceeding to be amended so that it may be disposed of on the
merits." Appellant respectfully requests leave to amend the notice of appeal to
designate this Court, and has filed an Amended Notice of Appeal in the lower tribunal
reflecting the correct court (see ¶ 8).

6. **In the alternative, transfer — not dismissal — is the remedy.** If this Court were
to conclude that review lies elsewhere, Florida Rule of Appellate Procedure 9.040(b)(1)
provides that "[i]f a proceeding is commenced in an inappropriate court, that court
shall transfer the cause to an appropriate court." Dismissal would be improper where a
timely notice of appeal was filed in the lower tribunal.

7. **The filing fee is being addressed separately.** Pursuant to this Court's separate
June 26, 2026 order, Appellant has filed [FILED / IS FILING] an Application for
Determination of Civil Indigent Status with the clerk of the lower tribunal under
section 57.081, Florida Statutes, and Rule 9.430, and will transmit the resulting
certificate or order to this Court [OR: has forwarded the $300 filing fee].

8. On July [___], 2026, Appellant filed an **Amended Notice of Appeal** in the lower
tribunal correcting the designation of the reviewing court to this Court.

WHEREFORE, Appellant respectfully requests that this Court accept jurisdiction of this
appeal, discharge the order to show cause, grant leave to amend the notice of appeal to
the extent necessary, and permit this appeal to proceed on the merits; in the
alternative, Appellant requests transfer to the appropriate court pursuant to Rule
9.040(b)(1) rather than dismissal.
