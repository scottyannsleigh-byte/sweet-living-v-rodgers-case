Counsel:

**1. Ownership of the mobile home.** I am the owner of the mobile home located at 3929
Argyle Street, Zephyrhills, FL 33542 (the "Home"). The Final Judgment for Possession
entered June 18, 2026 itself finds that I "held valid title to the mobile home,"
citing *Green Tree Acceptance, Inc. v. Zimerman*, 611 So. 2d 608 (Fla. 2d DCA 1993).
Whatever the outcome of the pending appeal concerning possession of the lot, the Home
is my personal property.

**2. Demand regarding the Home.** Neither Sweet Living, Inc., nor Desmond Surujbali,
nor any agent (including Jack Bergert or Manuel Diaz) is authorized to occupy, use,
rent, lease, sell, encumber, alter, remove fixtures from, re-title, or dispose of the
Home. Section 723.062, Florida Statutes, authorizes at most removal of a mobile home
"into storage" upon execution of a writ; it does not transfer ownership. Please
confirm in writing within ten (10) days: (a) the current location and condition of the
Home; (b) that no person has occupied or altered it since January 2026; and (c) the
name and address of any storage facility if it has been or will be moved.

**3. Right to sell the Home in place.** Under section 723.058(1), Florida Statutes, a
park owner may not "den[y] or abridge[] the right of any mobile home owner … to sell
his or her mobile home within the park." Without waiving any position in the pending
appeal, I request in writing: (a) confirmation that Sweet Living, Inc. will not
obstruct a sale of the Home within the park to a purchaser subject to approval under
section 723.059 (which "may not be unreasonably withheld"); and (b) a copy of the
park's written purchaser-approval criteria.

**4. Access.** I request a date and time within the next fourteen (14) days for me
(with a witness) to access, photograph, and inventory the Home. Please propose two
alternatives in writing.

**5. Preservation of evidence.** Litigation is pending and further proceedings are
reasonably anticipated. Demand is made that Sweet Living, Inc., its owner, and its
agents preserve, and not delete, alter, or discard: the rent ledger and accounting for
lot 3929 (2024–present); all tenancy applications and approval/denial records for the
park (2023–present), including mine and the background report relied upon for the
December 31, 2025 denial; the park's rules, prospectus, and all amendments; all
communications concerning me, Sally Potes, or the Home (including email, text
messages, and the "payment portal" records); all keys and the lock-blocking device
installed on the Home; and any photographs or video of the Home or lot. Failure to
preserve may support spoliation remedies.

**6. No waiver.** Nothing in this letter waives, limits, or abandons any position,
right, claim, or argument in the pending appeal or otherwise, all of which are
expressly reserved.
