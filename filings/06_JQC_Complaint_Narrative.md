# JQC Complaint — facts section

**Judge:** Hon. Kent Compton, County Court Judge, Pasco County, Sixth Judicial Circuit,
Division 6-B (Robert D. Sumner Judicial Center, Dade City).
**Complainant:** Brandi Rodgers (defendant in Sweet Living, Inc. v. Rodgers, Pasco
County Case No. 2026-CC-001256).

1. I am the defendant in a mobile-home eviction case brought by Sweet Living, Inc.,
whose owner is Desmond Surujbali. At a hearing on [DATE — the hearing at which you
heard it], Judge Compton stated from the bench, in words or substance, that **"Desmond
needs his money,"** referring to the plaintiff corporation's owner by his first name. I
was present and heard the statement, and I described it under oath in a verified motion
filed June 24, 2026 (docket entry 48, ¶ l, in the case above).

2. On June 18, 2026, Judge Compton entered a judicial default and final judgment of
possession against me although my answers (filed March 10, March 16, and May 20, 2026),
my demand for jury trial, and my unruled motions — including a motion to quash service
challenging personal jurisdiction and a motion for an evidentiary hearing on whether a
tenancy existed — were on file. The judgment stated my pending motions were "abandoned."

3. The plaintiff has filed nineteen eviction cases in Pasco County Court, all assigned
to Judge Compton and all filed by the same attorney. I filed documentation of seventeen
of these cases in the court record in March 2026 (docket entry 13).

4. On the same day my case was defaulted (June 18, 2026), Judge Compton heard the same
plaintiff's eviction against my next-door neighbor (Sweet Living, Inc. v. Dotson, Case
No. 2026-CC-003437) and denied the plaintiff's claim in that contested case by order
signed June 22, 2026.

5. I believe the statement in paragraph 1, in the context of paragraphs 2–4, reflects
conduct inconsistent with the Code of Judicial Conduct's requirements of impartiality
and the avoidance of the appearance of partiality (Canons 2 and 3). I respectfully
request that the Commission review it.

**Attachments:** verified emergency motion (DIN 48); Final Judgment (DIN 44, 6/18/2026);
Dotson order (6/22/2026); docket printout.
