# Florida Bar complaint — facts section

**Attorney:** Keith William Wynne, Fla. Bar No. 898201, The Law Office of Keith Will
Wynne, Esq., 1001 E. Baker St., Suite 101, Plant City, FL 33563.
**Complainant:** Brandi Rodgers (defendant in Sweet Living, Inc. v. Rodgers, Pasco
County Case No. 2026-CC-001256; appellant in 2D2026-1666).

1. Mr. Wynne represents Sweet Living, Inc., which sued me for eviction for non-payment
of rent on February 18, 2026, alleging I was its tenant at 3929 Argyle St., Zephyrhills.

2. **His client had already denied in writing that I could be a tenant.** On December
31, 2025, Sweet Living, Inc. sent me a Notice of Denial of Tenancy stating my residency
application was not approved. The eviction complaint nonetheless pleaded a tenancy and
sought rent from me. (Both documents attached.)

3. **The predicate notice asserted a fact his client's own records contradict.** The
January 5, 2026 notice states I was "currently residing" at the property. I never
resided there: I was denied residency, the park's property manager installed a
lock-blocker, and I never occupied the home. My verified filings in the case detail
this. Chapter 723, Fla. Stat., provides a specific ground and procedure for a purchaser
who is not approved for tenancy (§ 723.061(1)(e)); the complaint instead pleaded
non-payment of rent by a tenant and was served with a Chapter 83 residential summons.

4. **His client demanded the same rent from two different people for the same lot.** A
February 5, 2026 5-day notice demanded $1,166 from the seller, Sally Potes, for the same
lot for which the complaint demanded the same $1,166 from me. (Attached.)

5. **The civil cover sheet he filed states the case involves allegations of sexual
abuse.** The cover sheet in this eviction case marks "yes" to that question. (Attached.)

6. I ask the Bar to review whether this conduct is consistent with the Rules of
Professional Conduct, including the rules on meritorious claims and candor toward the
tribunal.

**Attachments:** complaint, summons, and cover sheet (2/18/2026); denial letter
(12/31/2025); 7-day notice (1/5/2026); 5-day notices (2/3 and 2/5/2026); final judgment
(6/18/2026); my verified emergency motion (DIN 48).
