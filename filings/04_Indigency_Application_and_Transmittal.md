# STEP-BY-STEP: Appeal filing fee — indigency route (§ 57.081 / Rule 9.430)

## Step 1 — Get and file the official application (do not draft your own)

Rule 9.430(a) requires the **Supreme Court–approved "Application for Determination of
Civil Indigent Status"** filed with the **clerk of the lower tribunal** (Pasco County
Clerk), not with the DCA.

- Get the form from the Pasco Clerk (Robert D. Sumner Judicial Center, 38053 Live Oak
  Ave., Dade City, or www.pascoclerk.com) — ask for the **civil indigent status
  application for an appeal in case 2026-CC-001256, DCA case 2D2026-1666**.
- Fill it in completely: income, household size (include your adult special-needs son if
  he depends on you), assets, debts. Sign it — it is signed under penalty of perjury.
- File it with the Pasco Clerk and tell them it is for the **appellate filing fee** so
  the certificate references the appeal.

## Step 2 — What happens next

- The **clerk** determines indigency from the application (§ 57.082/57.081 procedure).
  If the clerk denies it, the denial must be in writing and you may ask the **county
  court** to review it (Rule 9.430(a)).
- You want to leave with (or receive) a **certificate or order of indigent/insolvent
  status** referencing the appeal.
