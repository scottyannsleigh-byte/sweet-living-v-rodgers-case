# Attorney General FDUTPA complaint — narrative

**Complainant:** Brandi Rodgers, 13721 94th Ave., Seminole, FL 33776, 727-621-6090,
brandisreallife@gmail.com
**Businesses complained of:** Sweet Living, Inc. (Doc. No. P06000079283) and its owner
Desmond Surujbali, 312 E. Bloomingdale Ave., Brandon, FL 33511 — operator of Zephyr
Mobile Home Park, Zephyrhills, FL, and other mobile home parks.

## What happened to me

1. In December 2025 I paid $18,000 (my life savings) for a mobile home inside Zephyr
Mobile Home Park. The park allowed the sale to close, then denied my residency
application in writing on December 31, 2025.

2. Florida law (§ 723.059, Fla. Stat.) says park approval may not be unreasonably
withheld and contemplates rescission of the purchase when approval is denied. Instead
of the sale being unwound and my money returned: I was locked out of the home I paid
for (a lock-blocker was installed by the on-site manager), and the park owner sued me
for **eviction for non-payment of rent as a "tenant"** — the same status it had just
denied me in writing (Pasco County Case No. 2026-CC-001256). A default judgment was
entered against me on June 18, 2026; the eviction leaves the park owner in control of
the home and lot while I lost the $18,000 and now have an eviction record.

3. The same company demanded the same $1,166 rent from the seller for the same lot
(February 5, 2026 notice), then sued only me.

## Why I believe this is a pattern, not a one-off

4. Sweet Living, Inc. has filed **19 eviction cases in Pasco County Court** (17
documented in my court filing of March 2026, docket entry 13; two more filed after
mine). Its owner also files evictions under his personal name, Desmond Surujbali, in
Pasco, Hillsborough, and Polk counties. [ATTACH the verified case list when complete.]

4a. The scale is documented in the State's own records: DBPR's published mobile home
park registry lists **ten parks (710 lots)** registered to "SURUJBALI, DESMOND"
personally across Hillsborough, Polk, and Manatee counties (Alafia Mobile Plaza,
Chapman's, Duffey's, Melody Acres 1 & 2, Pine Lake, Pine Oaks, Rainbow, Speer,
Strawberry Fields) — one of them fee-delinquent. The park where I bought my home
(Zephyr MHP, Pasco, project PRMZ001210) is listed with owner of record "CHANG, HYE
SUK" — not the company that sued me as the park's owner — and its DBPR fee status is
"Delinquent."

5. On June 18, 2026, the same company's eviction against my next-door neighbor (Case
No. 2026-CC-003437) was **denied** when she contested it — supporting my belief that
these cases do not survive scrutiny when actually defended.

6. I ask the Attorney General to review whether the pattern — permitting mobile-home
sales inside its parks to close, denying the purchasers' tenancy afterward, keeping the
purchase price unrefunded, and then using eviction filings against the denied
purchasers — is an unfair or deceptive trade practice under Chapter 501, Part II,
Florida Statutes.

**Attachments:** denial letter; notices; complaint and judgment; Dotson order; title and
payment records; case list.
