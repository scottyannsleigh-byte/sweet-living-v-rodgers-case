# Report of suspected title fraud — mobile home at 3929 Argyle St., Zephyrhills, FL 33542

**Reporting party:** Brandi Rodgers, 13721 94th Ave., Seminole, FL 33776, 727-621-6090,
brandisreallife@gmail.com
**Subject of report:** Sally Potes (seller of the mobile home)
**Prior report:** Pasco County Sheriff's Office report #2025-775609 (December 27, 2025)

## Facts

1. On December 5, 2025, I agreed to buy the mobile home at 3929 Argyle St., Zephyrhills,
FL 33542 from Sally Potes for $18,000, paying a $1,000 deposit that day (handwritten
receipt in my possession showing the $17,000 balance). On December 6, 2025, I paid the
$17,000 balance in cash, counted out in front of a third-party witness who has signed an
affidavit. My bank records show the December 6, 2025 withdrawal.

2. The Certificate of Title the seller provided and signed shows: (a) a **sale price of
$2,000** — not the $18,000 actually paid; (b) a **sale date of January 2, 2026** — not
the actual December 6, 2025 date; and (c) the official brands **"REBUILT"** and **"MAY
HAVE PREVIOUSLY BEEN DECLARED A TOTAL LOSS VEHICLE DUE TO DAMAGE."** The seller had
represented to me that the home had no damage history.

3. Section 319.33, Florida Statutes, addresses false statements and alterations in
title transactions. I am reporting the discrepancy between the actual transaction and
the figures and date written on the title, and the concealment of the branded history,
for whatever investigation and action you deem appropriate.

4. Related civil context (for completeness): the mobile home park's owner, Sweet
Living, Inc., denied my residency application after this sale closed and then sued me
for eviction (Pasco County Case No. 2026-CC-001256). I attempted to report the title
issue in person to the Zephyrhills Police Department on January 13, 2026 and have been
unable to obtain a report number.
