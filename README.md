## 🔑 Key verified findings (each documented in `research/` and `evidence/`)

1. **The judgment contradicts itself** — ¶1 finds Rodgers "held valid title" (its own
   cited case means title passed to her at the December 6, 2025 sale); ¶4 defaults her
   as a *tenant* for not depositing *rent* — without the evidentiary hearing that
   *Frey v. Livecchi* and *Grimm v. Huckabee* require.
2. **The jury demand could not be "abandoned"** — Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.430(d).
3. **No enforceable lot rental agreement could exist** — § 723.011 requires prior
   delivery of a DBPR-approved prospectus; the park instead *denied* her tenancy in
   writing, then sued her as a tenant.
4. **The plaintiff corporation doesn't own the park** — county deed records show
   **Desmond Surujbali personally** bought the park land in June 2020 for $2,000,000;
   the DBPR registry was never updated and the park's fees are delinquent.
5. **The companion case was won next door** — the same judge **denied** the same
   plaintiff's eviction against the contested neighbor (*Dotson*) the same day.
