## THE CASE IN FIVE VERIFIED SENTENCES

1. **The judgment contradicts itself:** ¶1 finds Brandi "held valid title" (its own
   citation, *Green Tree*, means title passed to her 12/6/2025) — then ¶4 defaults her
   as a *tenant* for not depositing *rent*, without the evidentiary hearing that
   *Frey v. Livecchi* and *Grimm v. Huckabee* make mandatory. Per se reversible.
2. **Her jury demand could not be "abandoned":** Rule 1.430(d) — a jury demand *"may
   not be withdrawn without the consent of the parties."* Demanded in a pleading,
   docketed "Jury Trial: YES," never ruled on, ignored. Per se reversible.
3. **The relationship they sued on can't legally exist:** § 723.011 — no *enforceable*
   lot rental agreement without prior delivery of a DBPR-approved prospectus. They
   delivered none; they *denied* her tenancy in writing 12/31/2025, then sued her as a
   tenant for rent.
4. **The plaintiff doesn't own the park — the man behind it does, personally.**
   County deed records (✅ verified 7/2): **Desmond Surujbali individually** bought the
   park land in **June 2020 for $2,000,000** (parcel 23‑26‑21‑0020‑00500‑0010; 2026
   value **$2.09M**); the DBPR registry was never updated (still lists prior owner
   Chang, fee status **Delinquent**). The corporation that swore it "owns" the park
   holds no title — and Surujbali personally owns **ten more parks (710 lots)**, so a
   money judgment collects (§ 55.10 liens in **Pasco**, Hillsborough, Polk, Manatee —
   including on the park itself).
5. **The same judge, same day, same park:** *Dotson* (next door) contested and **won**;
   Brandi was defaulted. (Note: Dotson was pleaded on a different ground — rule
   violation vs. non‑payment — so use it as merits context, never as a bias argument;
   evictions reach this judge by administrative assignment order, not by choice.)
