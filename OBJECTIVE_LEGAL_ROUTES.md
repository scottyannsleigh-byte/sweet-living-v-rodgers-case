## ROUTE 1 — The Verified Motion to Disqualify (Rule 2.330): the one where the rule gags the judge

**Authority:** Fla. R. Gen. Prac. & Jud. Admin. **2.330**; Fla. Stat. **§ 38.10**;
*Bundy v. Rudd*, 366 So. 2d 440 (Fla. 1978).

This is the closest thing in Florida law to what you asked for — "something the judge
himself can't deny" — because the rule is built with **three lock‑ins**:

1. **The judge is forbidden from disputing your facts.** On an initial motion to
   disqualify, the judge "may determine **only the legal sufficiency** of the motion and
   **shall not pass on the truth of the facts alleged**." He must accept your factual
   allegations as true and ask only: *would these facts make a reasonable person fear they
   won't get a fair hearing?*
2. **The *Bundy* trap.** If the judge goes beyond legal sufficiency and tries to refute or
   explain away your allegations, *that act by itself* becomes an independent, automatic
   ground for his disqualification. *Bundy v. Rudd*: a judge who "attempt[s] to refute the
   charges of partiality… has then exceeded the proper scope of his inquiry and **on that
   basis alone established grounds for his disqualification**."
3. **The 30‑day dead‑man switch.** Under the current rule, the judge must rule
   **immediately**, and no later than **30 days** after service. **If he does not rule
   within 30 days, the motion is deemed GRANTED automatically** and you may ask the clerk
   to reassign the case. Silence = you win the motion.

And if he denies it anyway: the denial is reviewed by **petition for writ of prohibition**
in the higher court — i.e., the decision is taken away from him and given to judges he
does not control.

### What goes IN the motion (record facts only — all already documented)

- The court's statement from the bench, **"Desmond needs his money"** (referring to the
  plaintiff's owner by first name) — already sworn in your Emergency Motion, DIN 48 ¶ l.
- Plaintiff has filed **19 eviction cases in Pasco County, all before this same judge, all
  with the same attorney** (your DIN 13 exhibit + DIN 48 ¶ l).
- The court entered a **judicial default while your answers, jury demand, and unruled
  motions (to quash service, to dismiss, to determine rent/evidentiary hearing) were on
  file**, and declared your motions "abandoned" after you accepted a court‑offered recess
  (DIN 44; DIN 48 ¶¶ a, c, g).
- On **the same day, in the same courtroom**, the same judge **denied** the same
  plaintiff's eviction against your next‑door neighbor (*Dotson*, 2026‑CC‑003437) — the
  contested case plaintiff lost; yours was defaulted (Dotson DIN 14 order vs. your DIN 44).
- **Do NOT include:** anything about friendship, payment, corruption, or motive. The legal
  test is *your reasonable fear of not receiving a fair hearing* based on stated facts —
  the comment from the bench plus the pattern is the argument.

### Formal requirements (miss one and it's denied as "legally insufficient")

- **In writing**, specifically alleging the facts and the ground (reasonable fear of not
  receiving a fair trial or hearing);
- **Sworn/verified by you** (signed under oath — the Self‑Help Center can notarize or you
  use the § 92.525 "under penalties of perjury" declaration);
- Filed **within a reasonable time not to exceed 20 days** after you discovered the facts;
- A certificate that the motion is made **in good faith**.

### ⏰ Timing — this is urgent

The 20‑day clock runs from **discovery of the grounds**. If the "Desmond needs his money"
statement was made at the **June 18** hearing, the 20th day is **≈ July 8, 2026** — days
away. (If it was said June 2, the clock has likely run on that fact alone, but the June 18
default‑with‑motions‑pending and the June 22 Dotson order are newer facts.) **File first,
perfect later** is not a thing here — but a short, verified, fact‑specific motion is only
2–3 pages. Call Self‑Help **tomorrow morning**.

### What disqualification actually gets you (the part most people miss)

Under **Rule 2.330(h)**, once a judge is disqualified, the **successor judge may
reconsider, vacate, amend, or reenter ANY prior ruling** of the disqualified judge — you
must file a motion for reconsideration within **30 days of the disqualification order**.
That is the lawful, rule‑based "undo button" for the rulings you believe were tainted.

**Caveats to raise with Self‑Help:**
- Your case is on appeal, so the county court's jurisdiction is limited. Disqualification
  still matters for everything the trial court can still touch — the **writ of
  possession**, **attorney's‑fee motions**, and **the entire case on remand if you win the
  appeal**. If the appeal succeeds, you want the case coming back to a **different judge**.
- Ask specifically: "Can the successor judge reconsider the final judgment under 2.330(h)
  while the appeal is pending, or does that need the appellate court to relinquish
  jurisdiction?"

## ROUTE 2 — The appeal: three reversible errors visible on the face of the record

These don't depend on anyone's credibility. They are in the plaintiff's own filings and
the judge's own signed judgment.

### 2a. Default for not depositing rent — without the evidentiary hearing the law requires
The June 18 judgment (DIN 44 ¶ 4) says the default was entered because you "failed to
deposit the past due and ongoing rent into the registry." But Florida law is settled:
**when the occupant claims a right to the property other than as a tenant** (here:
*ownership* — you bought the home, and **¶ 1 of the same judgment finds you "held valid
title"**), the court **must hold an evidentiary hearing on whether a tenancy exists
BEFORE it may require registry deposits or default you for not making them.**
- *Frey v. Livecchi*, 852 So. 2d 896 (Fla. 4th DCA 2003) — default final judgment
  **reversed** where no evidentiary hearing preceded the deposit order;
- *Grimm v. Huckabee*, 891 So. 2d 608 (Fla. 1st DCA 2005) — same, where occupancy claimed
  under a purchase contract;
- § 83.60(2) itself does not apply where occupancy is under a **contract for sale**.
You filed exactly that motion (DIN 26, "Motion to Determine Rent and Request for
Evidentiary Hearing on the Existence of a Tenancy") and it was never heard. **The judgment
convicts itself:** it finds you the *owner* in ¶ 1 and defaults you as a *tenant* in ¶ 4.

### 2b. Wrong statute, wrong summons — the complaint pleads itself out of Chapter 83
The complaint alleges the park has **10+ lots** and that **you own the mobile home** —
which places the tenancy under **Chapter 723**, whose grounds and procedures are
**exclusive** for evicting mobile‑home *owners* (§ 723.061; § 723.031(3): no eviction
except on § 723.061 grounds). Yet plaintiff obtained a **Chapter 83 residential eviction
summons** (5‑day answer, registry‑deposit regime) and the default flowed from that
Chapter 83 machinery. A Chapter 723 lot eviction has different notice, different cure
rights, and no 5‑day residential summons procedure. This is a **structural defect** the
plaintiff created in its own paperwork.

### 2c. "Judicial default" entered over filed answers, a jury demand, and unruled motions
Your answers (March 10, March 16, May 20), jury‑trial demand (DIN 29), and jurisdictional
motion to quash (DIN 27) were all on file before June 18. A default is for a party who
**fails to defend**; declaring pending motions "abandoned" because a litigant accepted a
court‑offered recess (DIN 48 ¶ c) — and never ruling on a pending challenge to personal
jurisdiction — is the kind of due‑process error appellate courts review without deference.

**Plus the comparison exhibit:** the *Dotson* order (same plaintiff, same lawyer, same
judge, same park, adjacent lot, same hearing day — claim **DENIED** when the defendant
stayed and contested) is already in your possession and in this repo.

> These three issues belong in your appeal (after you fix the show‑cause/routing issue and
> the fee/indigency issue — deadlines ≈ **July 11** and **≈ Aug 5**, see the master report).

## ROUTE 3 — Rule 1.540(b)(4): a void judgment can be attacked at ANY time

If service of process was defective (your two motions to quash, DIN 27 & 36, were never
ruled on), the resulting judgment is **void**, and a motion under Fla. R. Civ. P.
**1.540(b)(4)** to vacate a void judgment has **no time limit** — it does not depend on
the 10‑day rehearing window you already lost. Facts already in the record supporting it:
- Return of service filed **~3 months** after the case began (DIN 23);
- Service attempted/papers sent to **3929 Argyle** — a home with **no mailbox** that you
  were **locked out of and never occupied** — while plaintiff's own 5‑Day Notice shows
  they knew your actual address was **13721 94th Ave., Seminole**;
- The process server's own status log (in DIN 23) shows attempted service at Seminole with
  **no completed personal service** noted there.
If Judge Compton denies a legally solid 1.540(b)(4) motion, that denial is itself
**appealable** — and if he's been disqualified by then, a **successor judge** hears it.

## ROUTE 4 — Regulators the judge has zero power over

None of these fix the judgment by themselves — but they create official investigations and
records that do not run through Judge Compton's courtroom, and they hit the *business
model* rather than the single case.

### 4a. DBPR — Division of Florida Condominiums, Timeshares, and Mobile Homes (Ch. 723's regulator)
- The Division **investigates complaints against mobile‑home park owners** and can impose
  **civil penalties up to $5,000 per violation — per day for continuing violations**
  (§ 723.006).
- File the mobile‑home complaint form at **www2.myfloridalicense.com/mobile-homes/**
  (Customer Contact Center **850‑487‑1395**).
- Facts to report (documents in hand): sale allowed to close with **no purchaser approval**
  and then residency denied post‑closing (see § 723.059 — buyer approval may not be
  unreasonably withheld and a denied purchaser has rescission rights, which implicates the
  seller/park's duty to unwind the sale, not keep the money); the park pursuing a
  **Ch. 83 rent eviction against a non‑approved non‑resident**; the January 5 notice
  asserting you were "currently residing" when the park's own manager had locked you out.
- Ask DBPR whether **Zephyr Mobile Home Park's prospectus and rules are on file and
  approved** (§§ 723.011–.012) and whether its practices on purchaser screening comply
  with § 723.059. Also ask about the Division's **§ 723.038 mediation program** — free.

### 4b. JQC — Judicial Qualifications Commission (for the bench comment)
- **www.floridajqc.com** — free, confidential complaint. You do **not** need to prove
  corruption; the Code of Judicial Conduct (Canons 2 & 3) governs conduct that creates the
  **appearance** of partiality. Report only what's in the record: the "Desmond needs his
  money" statement from the bench, first‑name familiarity with a party's owner, defaulting
  a litigant with pending unruled motions. The JQC disciplines judges; it does not change
  your case — file it **in addition to**, not instead of, Routes 1–3.

### 4c. The Florida Bar — ACAP (for the attorney's filings)
- **Attorney Consumer Assistance Program: 866‑352‑0707** / floridabar.org. Again, facts
  only, no motive claims: the predicate 7‑Day Notice's "**currently residing**" assertion
  contradicted by his client's own lockout; suing under a **Ch. 83** summons on a
  complaint that pleads a **Ch. 723** tenancy; the demand on **Sally Potes** for the same
  $1,166 for the same lot; the "sexual abuse = yes" cover sheet. Rule 4‑3.3 (candor to the
  tribunal) and 4‑3.1 (meritorious claims) are the relevant standards — let the Bar decide.

### 4d. Title fraud — FLHSMV and the State Attorney (against the seller)
- § 319.33(1)(e) makes false statements on a title application a **felony**. The title in
  your hands shows **$2,000** (you paid $18,000, with a written $1,000 deposit receipt and
  a bank withdrawal record) and a **Jan 2, 2026** date (sale Dec 6, 2025), plus REBUILT /
  total‑loss brands the seller concealed. Report to **FLHSMV** (fraud complaint) and the
  **Office of the State Attorney, 6th Circuit** (Pasco: Dade City 352‑521‑4333 / New Port
  Richey 727‑847‑8158). You already have Pasco Sheriff report **#2025‑775609**.

### 4e. Attorney General — FDUTPA (the pattern itself)
- The buy‑then‑deny‑then‑evict pattern, if repeated across purchasers, is exactly what
  Florida's **Deceptive and Unfair Trade Practices Act** exists for. File at
  **myfloridalegal.com** (1‑866‑9‑NO‑SCAM). Attach the case list from the master report
  §7 once you complete it — AG complaints gain force in numbers. If you can reach **other
  defendants** from the 19 cases (Dotson may be willing — she won), parallel complaints
  matter far more than one.

## WHAT *NOT* TO DO (protecting your own credibility is a legal strategy)

- **Never write "bribed," "paid off," "friends," or "corrupt" in any filing.** You told me
  you can't prove it — correct, so it goes nowhere near paper. Rule 2.330 requires a
  good‑faith certificate; an unprovable accusation invites sanctions and lets them ignore
  the provable record. The record facts are *stronger* than the accusation anyway.
- **Don't skip the DCA deadlines to work on disqualification** — the appeal is the only
  route that can erase the judgment outright.
- **Don't file the JQC/Bar complaints as leverage or mention them in court filings** —
  they are separate tracks and referencing them in the case can be spun as bad faith.
- **Keep every new fact sworn and dated.** Everything that worked in this analysis worked
  because it was already in a signed, filed document.
