## 🚩 Three Things to Raise With Self-Help / An Attorney

> These are observations from the public docket, **not legal conclusions**. A licensed attorney must tell you whether any of these are valid grounds for relief.

### Flag #1 — Service of Process
- The **Return of Service (Doc 23)** was not filed until **05/19/2026** — roughly **three months** after the case started.
- You filed **two** motions to quash service (Docs 27 and 36).
- **Question for Self-Help:** "If service of process was defective or late, what is the effect on every order entered before the return was filed? How does it affect a default?"

### Flag #2 — Default Entered After You'd Already Answered
- You filed an **Answer on 05/20/2026 (Doc 24)**.
- The court entered a **Default by Court on 06/03/2026 (Doc 43)** — two weeks AFTER your answer was on file.
- A clerk's default normally means "no response on file." A default entered when an answer exists is unusual.
- **Question for Self-Help:** "Can a default be entered after an answer is on file? If not, what is the proper motion to challenge the default and every judgment flowing from it?"

### Flag #3 — The Appeal Bond / Jurisdiction Question
- The DCA's show-cause order (Doc 52) asks why the appeal shouldn't be dismissed **"for lack of jurisdiction."**
- In Florida **eviction appeals**, the most common cause of a jurisdictional dismissal is a **missing or defective appeal bond** (Fla. Stat. § 83.59; Fla. R. App. P. 9.310 / 9.130).
- **Question for the Clerk (in person):** "Is there an appeal bond on file in case 512026CC001256CCAXES for the DCA appeal 26-1666? Show me the bond record."
- **Question for Self-Help:** "If no bond was filed, can I still post one now? What is the procedure?"
