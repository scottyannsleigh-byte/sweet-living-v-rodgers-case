## ⚠️ THE MOST IMPORTANT THING I FOUND

There is **a contradiction between your Notice of Appeal and the DCA's orders**
that is the actual cause of the show-cause order — and it's fixable.

### Your Notice of Appeal (DIN 47) says:

> "Defendant... hereby appeals to the **Circuit Court of the Sixth Judicial
> Circuit**, in and for Pasco County, Florida..."

### But the DCA's Order (DIN 52) says your appeal is in the **Second DCA**:

> "Appellant shall show cause within fifteen days why this appeal should not
> be dismissed for lack of jurisdiction, as Appellant has failed to provide a
> copy of the order appealed as required by the Florida Rules of Appellate
> Procedure..."

### What's happening (in plain English)

You filed a Notice of Appeal intending to go to **Circuit Court** (the normal
county-court appeal path), but for some reason the appeal was routed to the
**Second District Court of Appeal (DCA)** instead. The DCA can't figure out
why it's there, because **you didn't include a copy of the order being
appealed** in the transmittal.

This is a **procedural/paperwork problem, not a substantive one.** It is
almost certainly fixable. The DCA is giving you 15 days to explain.

### What you need to find out immediately

Call the **Pasco County Clerk** at **(727) 847-2411** and ask:

1. *"Why is my appeal in the Second DCA instead of the Circuit Court? I
     filed a Notice of Appeal to the Circuit Court under Rule 9.110."*
2. *"Did the Notice of Appeal Transmittal Form (DIN 50) include the actual
     order being appealed?"*
3. *"What do I need to file to fix this?"*

The answer is likely one of:
- **Amended Notice of Appeal** clarifying the destination court
- **A copy of the order appealed** transmitted to the DCA
- **A motion to transfer** the appeal from DCA back to Circuit Court

## STRONGEST SUBSTANTIVE ISSUES IN YOUR CASE

These are pulled directly from your own filings and the court's orders.
They are the issues most likely to matter on appeal. They are NOT legal
advice — they are what your filings actually say, organized.

### Issue 1 — The court never ruled on your jurisdictional challenge
- You filed a **Motion to Quash Service of Process** (DIN 27, May 21, 2026).
- A motion attacking personal jurisdiction under Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(5)
  **cannot be ignored** — it must be ruled on.
- The June 18 Final Judgment (DIN 44) declared all your pending motions
  **"abandoned"** because you accepted a court-offered recess.
- **From your own emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ c):** *"A litigant who
  accepts a court-offered break has not abandoned her motions. Moreover,
  a pending jurisdictional challenge under Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(5)
  cannot be extinguished by a recess; it must be ruled upon."*

### Issue 2 — Plaintiff's own documents destroy their tenancy claim
- **Plaintiff's 5-Day Notice to SALLY POTES** (Feb 5, 2026) for the same
  property (3929 Argyle St.) claimed Sally Potes owed $1,166 in rent.
- The complaint (DIN 3) claims YOU owe $1,166 in rent at the same address.
- **There can only be one tenant on one lot.** By demanding rent from
  Sally Potes, plaintiff legally acknowledged she — not you — was the
  responsible party. Plaintiff never sued Sally Potes.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ j):** *"This selective
  prosecution of Defendant while acknowledging Sally Potes as the actual
  responsible party demonstrates that no valid tenancy relationship
  existed between Plaintiff and Defendant."*

### Issue 3 — Rent from Dec 1, 2025 is factually impossible
- Complaint (DIN 3, ¶ 5) alleges rent owed starting **Dec 1, 2025**.
- Per your sworn affidavit, the $17,000 purchase was paid **Dec 6, 2025**.
- You could not owe rent on a property you didn't know existed until 5
  days after the alleged rent start date.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ k):** *"No rent was or could
  have been owed by Defendant for December 2025."*

### Issue 4 — No evidentiary hearing on existence of tenancy
- You filed a **Motion to Determine Rent and Request for Evidentiary
  Hearing on the Existence of a Tenancy** (DIN 26).
- Cited **Frey v. Livecchi, 852 So. 2d 896 (Fla. 4th DCA 2003)** and
  **Grimm v. Huckabee, 891 So. 2d 608 (Fla. 1st DCA 2005)**.
- The court entered final judgment **without conducting this hearing**.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ a):** *"This is reversible error."*

### Issue 5 — The court ignored its own prior order
- At the **March 24, 2026 zoom hearing**, Judge Compton stated on the
  record he had not yet seen the Certificate of Title and ordered
  in-person appearance to review evidence.
- At the June 18, 2026 hearing, the court **refused to review your
  certified complete title history from the Florida DMV**.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ h):** *"The Court created the
  conditions for a proper evidentiary hearing and then failed to conduct
  one. This is a direct contradiction of the Court's own prior order."*

### Issue 6 — Sworn eyewitness testimony ignored
- Your eyewitness (per the court's own March 24 order) appeared and was
  sworn in. They witnessed the $17,000 cash payment to Sally Potes on
  Dec 6, 2025.
- The June 18 Final Judgment makes **no reference** to this sworn
  testimony.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ i):** *"Ignoring sworn
  eyewitness testimony without explanation is reversible error."*

### Issue 7 — Title fraud
- The Certificate of Title from Sally Potes bears **"REBUILT"** and
  **"MAY HAVE PREVIOUSLY BEEN DECLARED A TOTAL LOSS VEHICLE DUE TO
  DAMAGE"** brands.
- Sally Potes represented no damage had occurred.
- Title shows **fraudulent sale price of $2,000** when you paid $18,000.
- Title shows **fraudulent date of Jan 2, 2026** when sale was Dec 6, 2025.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ m):** cites **Fla. Stat. §
  319.33(1)(e)** — making a false statement in a title application is
  a crime.

### Issue 8 — Jury trial demand ignored
- You filed a **Demand for Jury Trial** (DIN 29, May 21, 2026).
- The June 18 Final Judgment does not address or rule on it.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ g).**

### Issue 9 — Predicate notice is false
- The **7-Day Notice of January 5, 2026** falsely states you are
  *"currently residing"* at 3929 Argyle St.
- Per your filings, you never entered, never possessed, and were locked
  out by a lock-blocker installed by plaintiff's own property manager.
- **From your emergency motion (DIN 48, ¶ e):** *"An eviction proceeding
  based on a false predicate notice is defective from inception."*
