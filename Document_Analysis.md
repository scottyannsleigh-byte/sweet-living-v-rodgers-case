### What this means in plain English
- The DCA is **NOT** (yet) dismissing your appeal.
- They're saying: *"You didn't pay the $300 appeal filing fee. Pay it within 40 days, OR get a certificate saying you're indigent, or we may dismiss."*
- **The deadline is 40 days from June 26, 2026 = approximately August 5, 2026.** (Not July 11 — that was my earlier mistake about a different order.)

### Your two paths
1. **Pay $300** to the Second DCA Clerk, OR
2. **File for indigent (insolvent) status** under § 57.081. If you can't afford a lawyer, you almost certainly qualify for this. The lower tribunal (Pasco County Court) needs to issue the certificate.

**This is the single most important thing in your case right now.** Don't miss it.

## Major discrepancies found in the Plaintiff's filings

Reading Docs 2, 3, 4, 5 reveals the following. These are factual conflicts **on the face of the plaintiff's own documents.** None of this requires me to know anything but what the documents themselves say.

### Discrepancy #1 — Cover Sheet says "sexual abuse allegations" (Doc 2)
On the Civil Cover Sheet, Section IX asks: **"DOES THIS CASE INVOLVE ALLEGATIONS OF SEXUAL ABUSE?"** The box marked is **"yes."**

This is an **eviction over unpaid rent.** There are no sexual abuse allegations in the complaint. This appears to be an error by plaintiff's counsel (Keith William Wynne, Bar #898201) — but it's on the official court record and could affect how the case was processed, assigned, and flagged.

### Discrepancy #2 — The Complaint is internally contradictory about the tenancy type
The Complaint (Doc 3) **contradicts itself** on what kind of eviction this is:

- **Title of complaint:** "COMPLAINT FOR EVICTION — **MOBILE HOME PARK EVICTION** (Non-Payment of Rent)"
- **Body:** Alleges it's an action under **§ 723.061(1)(c)(1)** (the Mobile Home Park Act), that Plaintiff owns a "Mobile Home Park that contains 10 or more mobile home lots," and that "**Defendant owns the mobile home** located at 3929 Argyle Street."

**But the Summons (Doc 5) is an "EVICTION SUMMONS / RESIDENTIAL"** — which is for **Chapter 83 residential tenancies**, NOT mobile home park lot rentals under Chapter 723.

This matters because:
- Chapter 723 (mobile home parks) and Chapter 83 (regular residential rentals) have **different notice requirements, different defenses, and different procedures.**
- Filing the wrong summons / wrong statutory framework is a substantive defect.

### Discrepancy #3 — The 5-Day Notice has TWO conflicting delivery methods checked
In Doc 4 (Exhibit A, the 5-Day Notice), both boxes are checked under "I certify that I delivered this notice...":

- ☒ "Left at the premises, in the absence of the tenant(s);" **AND**
- ☒ "Sent by certified mail. (Add 5 days to payment deadline if mailed)."

These are **two different methods** with **two different legal effects** — posting adds no days, certified mail adds 5 days to the response deadline. Checking both creates ambiguity about whether the notice was actually, properly served.

### Discrepancy #4 — Address mismatch between 5-Day Notice and your actual location
- 5-Day Notice (Doc 4) is addressed to: **13721 94th Ave., Seminole, FL 33776** (Pinellas County)
- The property at issue is: **3929 Argyle St., Zephyrhills, FL 33542** (Pasco County)

The notice claims it was "left at the premises" (3929 Argyle) — but it's addressed to a different county. If the notice wasn't properly posted or mailed to your actual residence, **service of the statutory notice may be defective.**

### Discrepancy #5 — Plaintiff's address is wrong/incomplete in the notice
Doc 4 lists the Plaintiff's address as **"312. E. Bloomingdale Ave., Brandon, Fl 33811."** The period after "312" and the missing suite number suggest this was sloppily prepared. The attorney's cover letter address (Plant City) is also different from the Plaintiff's stated business address (Brandon). Minor, but it contributes to a pattern of carelessness that matters when assessing the reliability of the rest of their filings.

### Discrepancy #6 — Complaint demands "past due rent" but case is styled "non-monetary"
- The docket describes this case as "**Residential Eviction Non-Monetary**."
- But the Complaint (Doc 3, ¶ 6) demands "**judgment for past due rent**" and the 5-Day Notice (Doc 4) claims **$1,166.00 owed.**
- A "non-monetary" eviction is one where the landlord is **only** seeking possession, not money. If the complaint demands rent, the case classification on the docket is inconsistent with the relief sought.
