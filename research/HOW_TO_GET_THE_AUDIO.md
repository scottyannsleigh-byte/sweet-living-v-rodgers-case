   is the raw material a transcriptionist certifies a transcript FROM; (b) it
   supports the recusal motion's factual basis; (c) it anchors a Rule 9.200(b)(5)
   statement if a formal transcript is impractical.

## THE PROCESS AND COSTS (from the circuit's instructions, verbatim rules)

- Complete the request form; work begins on **receipt of a 50% deposit** of estimated
  cost; payment to **"STATE OF FLORIDA"** (check/money order; cards in person only).
- Transcripts produced **within 30 days** of deposit.
- **Indigency:** *"If you or your client has been found indigent for costs, please
  provide a court order granting transcription to be paid by the Justice
  Administrative Commission."* → When filing the indigency application (`filings/04`),
  ask the clerk/Self-Help how to obtain an **order granting transcription at public
  expense** for the appeal. This is a real, established path — use it.
- **Appeals:** provide the Designation to the court reporter (`filings/15`); the
  original + copies go to the Clerk of Court, Appeals, for the record.

## IF THE ANSWER IS "NO RECORDING EXISTS" — the fallback IS a real path

**Rule 9.200(b)(5), statement of the evidence or proceedings:** prepare a written
statement of what happened at each hearing **from the best available means, including
recollection**; serve it on Sweet Living's counsel (who may serve objections or
proposed amendments); then the **trial court settles and approves it**, and it goes
into the record. Practical notes:
- Draft one statement per hearing (March 24, June 2, June 18), each short and factual:
  who appeared, what was said about the title/evidence review, the jury demand, the
  recess, and the rulings announced.
- Two people heard the June 18 hearing from your side (your mom and the sworn
  eyewitness from the March 24 order) — both recollections can inform the statement.
- Yes, the judge who is the subject of the recusal motion settles the statement —
  if he alters or refuses key parts, that itself becomes reviewable, and the
  disqualification track (filing 01) runs in parallel for exactly this reason.

## WHAT DOES NOT EXIST ONLINE (so nobody wastes hope)

- Florida county-court Zoom civil hearings are **not** published to public archives;
  the circuit's YouTube/streaming was never a county-civil archive, and no recording
  of these hearings is publicly posted anywhere (checked).
- The Zoom meeting IDs in the notices are the court's meeting rooms, not recordings —
  they cannot be "replayed."
- The only lawful sources are: the court reporting department (ask — filing 16), the
  judge's own chambers recording if any (the JA call Monday can confirm), and the
  9.200(b)(5) statement path.
