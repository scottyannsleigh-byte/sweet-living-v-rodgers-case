### The two DCA orders — read them exactly
- **DIN 52 (Show Cause, June 26):** *"Appellant shall show cause within fifteen days why this
  appeal should not be dismissed for lack of jurisdiction, as Appellant has failed to provide a
  copy of the order appealed…and this court is unable thereby to determine its jurisdiction."*
- **DIN 53 (Filing Fee, June 26):** *"Appellant shall forward the required $300.00 filing fee
  or…a certificate or order of the lower tribunal finding Appellant insolvent pursuant to section
  57.081…within 40 days."*

**Two different deadlines — do not confuse them:**

| Deadline | Order | ~Date | What it needs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **~15 days → ~July 11, 2026** | DIN 52 | from June 26 | Explain jurisdiction + **attach the order you're appealing** (the June 18 Final Judgment) |
| **~40 days → ~August 5, 2026** | DIN 53 | from June 26 | **Pay $300** *or* file for **indigency** under § 57.081 |

> **Root problem — CORRECTED after verification (July 2, 2026):** an earlier version of this
> paragraph (and the older notes in this repo) said county‑court appeals belong in the
> **Circuit Court**. That was **pre‑2021 law**. Chapter 2020‑61, Laws of Florida, amended
> **§ 26.012, Fla. Stat.** effective **January 1, 2021**: county‑court final judgments are now
> appealed **directly to the DCA**. **The Second DCA is the CORRECT court.** The defect is that
> your Notice of Appeal (DIN 47) *said* "Circuit Court" and the DCA never received a copy of the
> judgment — both fixable: attach the judgment to your show‑cause response and file an Amended
> Notice of Appeal (see `filings/02` and `filings/03`). This is a **paperwork fix**, not a merits
> loss.

## 9. CONSOLIDATED DISCREPANCIES IN THE PLAINTIFF'S OWN PAPERS

Each of these is visible **on the face of the plaintiff's documents** (facts), independent of your
account:

1. **Wrong statutory vehicle:** complaint = **Ch. 723 mobile‑home** eviction; summons =
   **Ch. 83 residential**. Different notices, defenses, and procedures.
2. **5‑Day Notice served two ways at once:** both "left at premises" **and** "certified mail"
   checked (different legal effects on the deadline).
3. **Notice addressed to the wrong county:** the 5‑Day Notice is addressed to **13721 94th Ave.,
   Seminole (Pinellas)** while claiming it was "left at the premises" at **3929 Argyle
   (Pasco)**.
4. **Rent math doesn't add up:** $558 × 2 months (Dec + Jan) = **$1,116**, but the notice
   demands **$1,166**. The $50 is unexplained.
5. **"Non‑monetary" styling vs. a money demand:** the docket calls this non‑monetary, yet the
   complaint seeks past‑due rent.
6. **Rent from Dec 1, 2025 is impossible:** your bank record shows the **$17,000 paid Dec 6,
   2025**; you didn't know the property existed on Dec 1.
7. **Denial vs. "currently residing":** the **Notice of Denial of Tenancy** is dated
   **12/31/2025** (you're **not** approved), yet the **7‑Day Notice (1/5/2026)** says you are
   **"currently residing"** there — which your filings say never happened (lock‑blocker, no keys,
   no possession).
8. **Selective prosecution:** a **prior 5‑Day Notice demanded the same $1,166 from Sally Potes**
   for the same lot. They sued **you**, never Potes — undercutting who the real "tenant" was.
9. **Title fraud indicia:** the certificate of title bears **"REBUILT"** and **"MAY HAVE
   PREVIOUSLY BEEN DECLARED A TOTAL LOSS"** brands, shows a **$2,000** price (you paid $18,000)
   and a **Jan 2, 2026** date (sale was Dec 6, 2025). You cite **Fla. Stat. § 319.33(1)(e)**.
10. **Cover‑sheet error:** the Civil Cover Sheet marks **"yes"** to *"allegations of sexual
    abuse"* in an eviction — a filing error, but it's on the official record.
11. **Notice address ≠ the corporation's registered address:** Sweet Living, Inc.'s official
    Division of Corporations **principal address is 11888 US Hwy 41 S, Gibsonton, FL 33534**;
    the notices use "312 E. Bloomingdale Ave., Brandon" (mistyped "33811"). (See
    `SWEET_LIVING_BYLAWS_AND_CORPORATE_RECORDS.md` §3.)
12. **Denial signed by a non‑officer:** the corporation's **only director of record is Desmond

    His authority to bind the corporation is unestablished — a discovery target.
