## 2. THE TRANSCRIPT TRAP — the single biggest threat to the appeal

***Applegate v. Barnett Bank of Tallahassee*, 377 So. 2d 1150 (Fla. 1979)** (verified):
the trial court's decision arrives at the DCA **presumed correct**, and *"[w]ithout a
record of the trial proceedings, the appellate court cannot properly resolve the
underlying factual issues"* — meaning **no transcript ≈ affirmance**, no matter how
good the arguments, **except** where the error is **apparent on the face of the
record/judgment itself.**

**What this means for this appeal:**
- The four chosen issues were selected precisely because they are **face-of-record
  errors** (the judgment's own ¶1/¶4 contradiction; the docketed jury demand vs. ¶C
  "abandoned"; the filed DIN 26 vs. no hearing held; the complaint/summons mismatch).
  That is the *Applegate* exception lane — this is survivable even without transcripts.
- **But transcripts convert "survivable" into "strong":** the June 18 recording proves
  no evidentiary hearing occurred, no jury-waiver colloquy happened, and what was
  actually said. And the plaintiff will try to use the missing transcript against
  every argument.

**DO THIS FIRST (before the recusal motion, before anything):**
1. **Monday, July 6, when the court reopens:** ask the clerk/court administration how
   to order the **digital recordings and transcripts** of the **March 24, June 2, and
   June 18, 2026** hearings, AND
2. **File a "Designation to Court Reporter / Directions to Clerk" in the county court**
   designating those three hearings for transcription and inclusion in the record
   (Rule 9.200(b)). The 10-day window from the June 24 notice lands **≈ July 6** — if
   it has lapsed, file the designation immediately anyway with a short motion for
   leave to file out of time (routinely granted; ask Self-Help for the form).
3. **If a hearing was not recorded** (ask!): Rule 9.200(b)(5) allows a **"statement of
   the evidence or proceedings"** prepared from recollection, served on the other side,
   and settled by the trial court. Ask Self-Help about this the same day you learn any
   hearing is unrecorded.
4. **Indigency covers transcripts too** — raise it when filing the § 57.081
   application (`filings/04`).

## 3. ONE PRESERVATION NUANCE TO RAISE WITH SELF-HELP

Florida practice requires a **motion for rehearing** to preserve some errors that
appear *for the first time on the face of the final judgment* (e.g., inadequate
findings). No motion titled "rehearing" was filed — but **DIN 48 was filed June 24,
within 15 days of the June 18 judgment**, sworn, and raised every issue (the hearing
denial, the jury demand, the "abandoned" ruling). Ask Self-Help/any counsel: *"Should
the brief characterize DIN 48 as a timely post-judgment motion preserving the
face-of-judgment issues?"* The core issues were also all raised **before** judgment
(DINs 24, 26, 27, 29), which is independent preservation.

## 4. THE BRIEF — architecture (agreed, with one structural weld)

**Order of issues (lead with the most conventional, cleanest-remedy error):**

- **Issue I — The court defaulted her for non-deposit without the evidentiary hearing
  that *Frey* and *Grimm* required.** (Face of record: DIN 26 requested it citing both
  cases → DIN 41 denied it as "legally deficient" → DIN 44 ¶1 finds she "held valid
  title" — the very finding that triggers *Frey/Grimm* — then ¶4 defaults her as a

  for a panel to write.)
- **Issue II — The jury demand could not be "abandoned."** (Rule 1.430(d); § 83.60(1)
  "the court or the jury"; § 51.011(3); Art. I, § 22; *Pernell v. Southall Realty*,
  416 U.S. 363 (1974). **Weld:** answer the statutory-default counterargument INSIDE
  this issue by incorporating Issue III — no tenancy → § 83.60(2)/§ 723.063(2) deposit
  duty never attached → no lawful procedural default → the demand survived.)

  prerequisite; the 12/31/2025 written denial of tenancy; § 723.003(11); § 723.063(2)
  textually inapplicable. Short, statutory, feeds I and II.)
- **Issue IV — Wrong statutory vehicle.** (Ch. 723 complaint prosecuted through the
  Ch. 83 residential summons and deposit machinery — Form 1.923(a) on the face of the

## 5. ODDS, HONESTLY

Percentages in litigation are guesses wearing suits. What is real: **the issue type
here — preserved, face-of-record, per-se procedural error with two on-point DCA
cases — is the single category where pro se appeals actually win.** The three
controllable variables are (1) the transcript designation (this week), (2) a clean,
compliant, ruthlessly focused brief (eight weeks), and (3) not missing the July 10–13
and Aug 5 gates. Everything else is noise.
