## 1. THE STRAIGHT ANSWER ABOUT "BYLAWS"

**Corporate bylaws are internal documents. Florida does not require them to be filed
anywhere public**, so no online search can produce Sweet Living, Inc.'s bylaws — not
because they're hidden, but because the law keeps them in the company's own records
(only the *Articles of Incorporation* and *annual reports* are filed with the state).

**But that's fine — the bylaws are the least important governing document in your
case.** For a Chapter 723 mobile home park there is a set of governing documents that
**must** be filed with or produced to regulators and tenants, and *those* are the ones
their eviction cases stand or fall on:

| Document | Public? | Why it matters to you |
|---|---|---|
| **Park prospectus** (§ 723.011–.012) | **YES — filed with DBPR** (mandatory for parks with 26+ lots; Zephyr has **82**) | Must contain the park's rules, fee structure, and tenant-approval scheme |
| **Park Rules & Regulations** | **YES — part of the DBPR prospectus file** | Their 1/5/2026 notice quotes *"Paragraph 2 of the Zephyr Mobile Home Park Rules and Regulations."* A § 723.061(1)(c) eviction requires a **"properly promulgated"** rule — if the version they quoted was never properly filed/delivered, the predicate for their notice weakens |
| **Tenant screening/approval criteria** (§ 723.059) | Not filed, but **discoverable** | Approval "may not be unreasonably withheld" — the written criteria are how you test whether your denial was lawful |
| **55+ age-verification policy** (80/20 rule) | Not filed (registration repealed 2020), but **discoverable** | Zephyr advertises as a 55+ community — the fair-housing exemption requires written procedures they must be able to produce |
| **Corporate bylaws / articles** | Articles & annual reports: **YES (Sunbiz)**; bylaws: discovery only | Officers, authority to sign notices (who is "Manuel Diaz"?), corporate formalities |

  actually practiced there.

## 4. HOW TO PULL EACH RECORD (exact steps — all free or nearly free)

### A. The park's DBPR prospectus & rules file — **the big one**
1. Go to **www.myfloridalicense.com** → *Verify a License* → search **"Zephyr"** /
   organization, or call the **Division of Florida Condominiums, Timeshares & Mobile
   Homes: 850‑488‑1122** (2601 Blair Stone Rd., Tallahassee, FL 32399).
2. Ask, word for word: *"I need the **prospectus/registration file** for **Zephyr
   Mobile Home Park, 3950 Dixon Street, Zephyrhills, FL 33542** — the PRMZ project
   number, the currently approved prospectus, the currently approved **Rules and
   Regulations**, all amendments, and the park's annual fee/lot-count history. I'd
   like to make a **public records request** for copies."*
3. When you get the rules: compare **"Paragraph 2"** as filed with what their
   1/5/2026 notice quoted, check whether the sale-approval rule was in the *approved*
   version on the date of your purchase, and check the filed **tenant approval
   criteria** against § 723.059.
4. While you have them on the phone: ask about **free mediation under § 723.038** and
   how to attach this to your DBPR complaint (`filings/05`).

### B. Sunbiz corporate filings (officers year by year, articles)
1. Go to **sunbiz.org** → *Search Records* → *Search by Entity Name* → type
   **"Sweet Living"**.
2. Open **SWEET LIVING, INC. (P06000079283)**. The detail page lists officers and the
   registered agent, and at the bottom, **Document Images** — every annual report
   since 2006 and the original **Articles of Incorporation** as free PDFs. Download
   them all; they show who has been an officer each year (watch for names you know:
   Diaz? Potes? Bergert?).
3. While there, also open the **"SWEETLIVING…" LLC (L23000449630)** entry and any
   entity for **"Surujbali"** (*Search by Officer/Registered Agent name* → "Surujbali")
   — that officer search is how you map **all** his entities in one shot.

### C. Who owns the land (Pasco Property Appraiser + Clerk)
1. **search.pascopa.com** → *Search by Owner Name* → **"Sweet Living"**, then
   **"Surujbali"** — lists **every parcel** each owns in Pasco County, with values and
   sales history. Find the parcel(s) under Zephyr MHP (3950 Dixon St. area) and note
   the **exact titled owner**.
2. **Pasco Clerk Official Records** (pascoclerk.com → Official Records search, free) —
   search both names for **deeds, mortgages, and liens**. This shows when they bought
   the park and from whom, and any lender (a lender's estoppel/consent issues
   sometimes surface in park sales).
3. Repeat in **Hillsborough** (hillsclerk.com) and **Polk** (polkcountyclerk.net) if
   you want the full portfolio map for the AG complaint.

### D. The bylaws themselves + screening criteria — **discovery** (the courtroom route)
When the case is live again in the trial court (on remand, or in post-judgment
proceedings), a pro se party can serve a **Request for Production** (Fla. R. Civ. P.
1.350). Ask Self‑Help to help format one requesting:
1. Sweet Living, Inc.'s **bylaws** and any resolutions authorizing the notices signed
   by Manuel Diaz and Desmond Surujbali;
2. The **Rules and Regulations** of Zephyr MHP in force December 2025 – February 2026,
   and proof of their delivery/promulgation to homeowners;
3. The park's current **prospectus** as filed with DBPR;
4. The **written tenant screening/approval criteria** used under § 723.059, and every
   application file and approval/denial issued at the park from 2023–2026 (redacted as
   needed) — this is how "applies its written criteria uniformly" gets tested;
5. Your complete **application file**, including the background report relied on for
   the December 31, 2025 denial and the identity/qualifications of who made the
   decision;
6. The **rent ledger for lot 3929** (2024–2026) and all rent demands issued for that
   lot (this catches the Sally Potes double-billing in their own books);
7. All **communications with Sally Potes** about the lot, the sale, and the eviction.
   *(A non-party like Potes can be reached with a **subpoena duces tecum** — ask
   Self‑Help.)*

## 5. WHAT EACH DOCUMENT CAN PROVE (connecting it to the case)

| If the record shows… | It supports… |
|---|---|
| No approved prospectus/rules on file, or "Paragraph 2" differs from the filed version | The § 723.061(1)(c) predicate notice was not based on a **properly promulgated** rule |
| Screening criteria that weren't applied uniformly (or don't exist in writing) | § 723.059 "unreasonably withheld" approval → your denial-of-tenancy was itself unlawful, undermining the whole eviction |
| Land titled to an entity other than Sweet Living, Inc. | The **standing** defect you raised in DIN 25 |
| Officer lists naming the people who signed your notices | Whether Diaz/others had authority; corporate sloppiness pattern |
| Rent ledger charging two people for one lot | The Sally Potes contradiction, in their own books |
| Parcel map of every park + the two-name case list | The **pattern** exhibit for the AG/FDUTPA complaint and the DCA |
